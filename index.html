Research

My mathematical interests lie in enumerative geometry, Gromov-Witten theory, and related aspects of mirror symmetry. The current focus of my research uses predictions from mathematical physics to relate Gromov-Witten invariants to other classes of algebro-geometric objects.

Wall crossing and the Fourier-Mukai transform for Grassmann flops
With Nathan Priddis and Yaoxiong Wen, SIGMA (2025).
We prove the crepant transformation conjecture for relative Grassmann flops over a smooth base B. We show that the I-functions of the respective GIT quotients are related by analytic continuation and a symplectic transformation.
Seiberg-like duality for resolutions of determinantal varieties

We study the genus-zero Gromov-Witten theory of two natural resolutions of determinantal varieties, termed the PAX and PAXY models.
